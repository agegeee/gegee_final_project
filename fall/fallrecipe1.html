stuffed eggplant parmesan

I might be deeply ambivalent about: * fall (less the weather cooling off and more how long it insists up staying cooled off for; doesn’t 8 months seem excessive?), * stuffed vegetables (the good ones are great but the bad ones a very underseasoned-rice-in-green-peppers-cooked-until-soggy-and-gray, you know?), * and eggplant parmesan (mostly the staggering amount of effort made to fry and crisp rounds of eggplant only to burrow it in crisp-cancelling sauce and cheese, why)

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 medium eggplants (about 3/4-pound each); use more if fairytale (small) eggplants
  • Olive oil
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely chopped (about 1 cup)
  • 1/2 pound ground sausage meat (I use mild Italian pork or chicken)
  • 1 1/2 cups tomato marinara sauce, prepared (use your favorite brand or the recipe in Notes)
  • 1 handful fresh basil leaves, torn
  • 2 tablespoons panko-style breadcrumbs
  • 6 ounces (about 1 1/3 cups) low-moisture mozzarella, coarsely grated, divided
  • 2 ounces (about 1/2 cup) finely grated parmesan, divided

Preheat your oven to 400°F. Cut each eggplant in half lengthwise. Use a paring knife to outline a 1/2-inch border around the eggplant half, then scoop out the flesh with a spoon or melon baller, ensuring you don’t cut through the bottom. This will create boats from the eggplant halves. Rub each half with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Arrange the eggplant halves cut side up in a 9x13-inch baking dish or a 3-quart casserole dish, and roast until the eggplant is tender and the edges are browned, about 25 to 30 minutes. Keep the oven on after roasting.

While the eggplant roasts, prepare the filling. Chop the scooped-out eggplant flesh into 1/2-inch chunks and set it aside. In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the onion and cook for 3 to 5 minutes, until translucent. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute. Add the sausage meat and break it up with a spoon, cooking until it’s no longer pink, about 3 to 4 minutes. Add the chopped eggplant, season with salt and black pepper, and cook until the eggplant is soft and begins to stick to the pan, about 7 minutes. Stir in 1/2 cup of marinara sauce and some of the basil, warming everything through. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Remove from heat and mix in half of the mozzarella and parmesan.

Once the eggplant boats are tender, remove them briefly from the dish and pour the remaining 1 cup of marinara sauce into the bottom of the dish. Stir the remaining basil into the sauce. Place the eggplant boats back into the dish and stuff them with as much filling as possible (you’ll likely have some leftover filling that can be baked in a smaller dish). Sprinkle the stuffed eggplants with the remaining mozzarella and parmesan. Drizzle a teaspoon of olive oil and a pinch of salt over the breadcrumbs, stirring to coat evenly, then sprinkle them over the cheese.

Bake the stuffed eggplants for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Then, broil for 2 to 5 minutes, or until the top is browned and blistered, depending on your oven’s broiler. Let the eggplants rest for 5 minutes before serving, spooning some extra sauce from the pan over each.

You can assemble this dish up to two days ahead of baking. It reheats well, keeping in the fridge for 2 to 4 days. It should also freeze well, although this hasn’t been tested.
